# Resources: Further Reading & Tools for Bioelectronic Medicine Architects

## Key Guidelines & Standards
- **ISO 14708 series** — Active implantable medical devices, general and particular requirements
- **ISO 10993 series** — Biological evaluation of medical devices
- **IEC 62304** — Medical device software lifecycle processes
- **IEC 60601 series** — Medical electrical equipment safety and performance
- **FDA Premarket Approval (PMA) guidance** and IDE regulations (fda.gov)
- **EU MDR (2017/745)** — active implantable device requirements
- **FDA Premarket Cybersecurity Guidance** (fda.gov)

## Textbooks & References
- **"Neuromodulation: Comprehensive Textbook of Principles, Technologies, and Therapies"** (Krames, Peckham, Rezai, eds.) — comprehensive field reference
- **"Neural Engineering"** (Bin He, ed.) — foundational neural engineering textbook covering stimulation and sensing principles
- **"Implantable Neural Prostheses"** (Zhou & Greenbaum, eds.) — electrode and circuit design reference

## Landmark Papers
- Tracey (2002) — "The inflammatory reflex" (Nature) — foundational bioelectronic medicine mechanism paper
- Famm et al. (2013) — "Drug discovery: a jump-start for electroceuticals" (Nature) — coined/popularized the electroceuticals concept
- Merrill, Bikson & Jefferys (2005) — "Electrical stimulation of excitable tissue: design of efficacious and safe protocols" (Journal of Neuroscience Methods) — foundational stimulation safety/charge density reference
- Melzack & Wall (1965) — "Pain mechanisms: a new theory" (Science) — original gate control theory paper underlying SCS mechanism

## Organizations
- **International Neuromodulation Society (INS)** — field-wide professional society and conference
- **North American Neuromodulation Society (NANS)** — regional professional society
- **Bioelectronic Medicine journal community** — dedicated field journal
- **IEEE EMBS (Engineering in Medicine and Biology Society)** — relevant conferences (EMBC)

## Regulatory Resources
- FDA Center for Devices and Radiological Health (CDRH) neurological device panel documentation (fda.gov)
- EU Notified Body guidance documents for active implantable devices
- IMDRF active implantable device-relevant working group publications
